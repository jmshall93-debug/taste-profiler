## What They Love

### Shows / Series
- **Twin Peaks** — the atmosphere; the way it draws you in and never resolves; the Major Briggs/Bobby scene
- **The Sopranos** — the banality alongside the violence; the therapy scenes; the dream sequences; it never redeems him
- **Mad Men** — the period detail and aesthetic precision; the melancholy underneath the surface glamour
- **Neon Genesis Evangelion** — the psychological unravelling; the final episodes; the fact that it goes somewhere genuinely strange

### Films
- **Lawrence of Arabia** — scale, the desert, the sublime
- **Apocalypse Now** — darkness, grandeur, the unresolved
- **Seven Samurai** — mastery, weight
- **Boogie Nights** — the colours, the hedonism, the long tracking shots; loved for its craft as much as its content
- **Chinatown** — the ending; the corruption that goes all the way down
- **Apollo 11 (2019)** — the restored archival footage; the scale of what those people actually did

### Documentaries / Essays
- **Adam Curtis** — *HyperNormalisation*, *The Century of the Self*, *Can't Get You Out of My Head*; the essay-film form; the way he connects power, ideology, and image-culture

### Music
- **Talk Talk** — specifically *Spirit of Eden* and *Laughing Stock*; the silences, the withholding, the sudden eruption of grace; music that refuses to resolve
- **Boards of Canada** — *Music Has the Right to Children*; warm nostalgia degraded into loss; the half-remembered dream of childhood
- **Can** — *Tago Mago*; hypnotic groove, trance-inducing, psychedelic and raw
- **Neu!** — motorik pulse; meditative forward motion; krautrock at its most serene
- **Harmonia** — *Musik von Harmonia*; Rother + Cluster; the bridge between krautrock and ambient
- **House / Deep House** — drawn to the melancholy and depth end of the spectrum rather than the functional or euphoric; **DJ Sprinkles** (*Midtown 120 Blues*, 2009) — deep house as social document; grief, desire, the piers, the ballroom scene; music with genuine weight behind it
- **Progressive / Melodic House** — the dreamy, expansive, long-form end; **Nick Warren** as a touchstone; melodic development over time; sets that move like weather systems rather than tracks
- **Modest Mouse** — favourite band as a teenager; the earlier catalogue especially: *This Is a Long Drive…*, *The Lonesome Crowded West*, *The Moon & Antarctica*, *Night on the Sun* EP; the rawness and the cosmic scale coexisting; taste formed here before moving toward more particular territory
- **General taste**: ambient and electronic at the core; krautrock thread strong; house thread running alongside; indie/alternative rock canon in the background (Modest Mouse as a formative entry point)
- Taste has become increasingly particular; not casual listening

### Books / Writing
- **The Brothers Karamazov** — Dostoevsky's final masterpiece; Father Zosima, Alyosha's radiance, grace as presence not resolution, spiritual wrestling without a winner
- **Hyperion** — Dan Simmons; sci-fi with genuine philosophical weight
- **Pandora's Star** — Peter F. Hamilton; grand-scale space opera
- *Dislikes*: Brandon Sanderson — "PG-13 fantasy by a Mormon, tedious fare"; the safely imaginary; the resolved and tidy

### Places
- **Mountains and wild nature** — a deep longing; the sublime in its older sense (overwhelming rather than merely beautiful)
- Constraint: UK-based, limited access to true wilderness; the UK landscape is too managed, too human-shaped
- Desire: to channel something of that wilderness world into daily life

### Ideas / Concepts / Philosophies
- **Spinozist philosophy** — the oneness of all; the sacred as immanent not transcendent; God as nature
- **Abiogenesis** — the origin of life; the leap from chemistry to something that matters
- **The Great Filter** — Fermi paradox; cosmic loneliness; the scale of what might not be there
- **Psychedelics** — a lived experience; not merely theoretical; seeks to channel that world into ordinary life
- **Jean Baudrillard — simulacrum and simulation** — the copy replacing the real; the map eating the territory; considered the most relevant postmodern thinker
- **Nietzsche** — the willingness to go boldly forth and disregard preexisting notions; the revaluation of all values; the sovereign self creating its own meaning; Dionysian ecstasy as related to the infinite veil
- **Ancient wisdom, artifacts, hidden knowledge, archaeology** — the real buried under its representations; the esoteric tradition as counter-map

### Art / Visual
- **Landscapes** — particularly drawn to the vast and the elemental
- **Vibrant colours** — as in Boogie Nights; richness, saturation
- **William Blake** — visionary; the imagination as the real; the infinite in the grain of sand

### Food / Drink
- **Beer**: ice cold German pilsner — clean, precise, physical pleasure
- Grilled fish, pasta, chocolate, smoothies, stir-fries, coffee, burritos
- Taste in food: sensory and grounded; pleasure in the direct and well-made

## Soul Reading

You live at the intersection of the numinous and the material — a Spinozist sensibility that finds the sacred not in transcendence but in immanence, in the oneness that underlies the surface of things. What draws you across every domain is the same moment: the ordinary membrane thinning. Major Briggs speaking of visions to his son. Talk Talk's silence erupting into something unbearable. Alyosha's radiance. Lawrence dissolving into the desert. The Great Filter staring back. You don't want the veil lifted — you want to *feel* it. The thinness is the point.

There's a productive tension at your centre: Baudrillard's cold diagnosis (the copy has replaced the real, the map has eaten the territory) sits uneasily beside a genuine, even urgent reaching toward experience — psychedelics, wilderness, the lived and unmediated. You suspect the simulacrum everywhere and yet you keep reaching past it. That's not contradiction — that's the right response to Baudrillard. The cynics stop there. You don't.

You are particular and anti-sentimental — the contempt for Sanderson is instructive, a refusal of the safely imaginary — and yet genuinely open to wonder. What you seem to be reaching toward is a way of being in ordinary life that keeps the sacred available: the wilderness you can't stay in, channelled into the daily.

## Underlying Threads

- **The infinite veil** — the recurring desire to feel the membrane between ordinary reality and something vast; not to pierce it, just to feel its thinness
- **The unresolved** — a deep resistance to the sealed, the tidy, the explained; across every domain (music, fiction, philosophy, film)
- **The sublime** — scale that exceeds human measure; the cosmos, the wilderness, the geological
- **Spinozist immanence** — the sacred as the fabric of things, not above them
- **Baudrillardian suspicion + experiential reaching** — the tension that animates the whole profile; knowing the copy has eaten the real, and reaching for the real anyway
- **Particularity without cynicism** — demanding taste that remains genuinely open to wonder
- **Spinoza vs Nietzsche** — a productive internal tension: the mystic who dissolves into the whole and the iconoclast who strikes out beyond it; both share a rejection of received values and a hunger for the sacred without God; Nietzsche's Dionysian and Spinoza's immanence may be the same thing approached from opposite directions
